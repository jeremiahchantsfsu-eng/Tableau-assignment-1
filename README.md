## AdventureWorks Cycles Sales Analysis (Tableau) Overview

This project analyzes historical sales and customer data for AdventureWorks Cycles (AWC) using Tableau. The objective was to uncover meaningful patterns in sales performance, profitability, and customer demographics and present insights through interactive visualizations and dashboards.

The dataset contains 60,399 records spanning July 2016 – August 2019, provided as an Excel extract from a SQL Server view.

## Tools Used

Tableau Desktop

Excel (data source provided)

## Key Visualizations

Sales Trends Over Time: Evaluates the impact of new bike models on average and total sales.

Global Sales Map: Highlights regional sales performance by country and city.

Product Profitability: Uses calculated fields for Profit and Gross Profit Margin to identify high- and low-performing products.

Customer Demographics: Analyzes purchasing behavior by income level and household characteristics.

Top Products: Identifies the highest-revenue product and the most frequently sold product.

Exploratory Analysis: Additional insights discovered through independent data exploration.

## Dashboard

A final interactive dashboard combines five key visualizations with shared filters for quick and easy analysis. The dashboard is designed to support business decision-making and clearly communicate insights.

## Business Questions Answered & Value to the Company

This analysis helps AdventureWorks Cycles answer several key business questions:

How product changes affect sales: Sales trends show how introducing mid-priced bike models and discontinuing high-end models impacted overall revenue and average sale value over time.

Where revenue is generated globally: Geographic visualizations identify top-performing countries, cities, and regions, helping prioritize markets for expansion, marketing, or inventory allocation.

Which products are most profitable: Profit and gross margin analysis reveals which products drive profit versus volume, supporting pricing, cost control, and product portfolio decisions.

Who the customers are: Demographic analysis shows how income level and household characteristics influence purchasing behavior, enabling more targeted marketing strategies.

What products matter most: Identifying the highest-revenue and most-sold products helps guide promotions, demand forecasting, and supply chain planning.

## Business Impact

By turning raw sales data into interactive dashboards, this project enables leaders to:

Make faster, data-driven decisions without relying on IT-generated reports

Focus resources on high-margin products and high-performing regions

Reduce risk by identifying trends and areas with limited impact

Improve strategic planning across sales, marketing, and operations
